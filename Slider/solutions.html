    PUBLIC’s LACK OF ENTHUSIASM FOR NEW HOUSING SCHEMES
    This often filters through into rejection of Planning Applications. They are often seen as too large -thus swamping the local community - and the environment they create is seen as unpleasant and alien with too many houses crammed together and too little open Space and vegetation

    Construct Hedge houses! These small scale structures, covered in vegetation will not only provide a welcome sight of nature in a built up area - especially with splendid colours in Spring and Autumn. They will also a give a home to birds, bees, butterflies and other important creatures. The reduction in many species numbers is not only due to the removal of natural habitats but also due to the increasingly violent weather caused by Climate Change. Hedge houses can give vulnerable species a home. The attractiveness of Hedge houses and what they can do for Nature will not be lost on the public and indeed they might start to see New Housing as helping Nature and thus in a very positive light: After all, new housing does have other beneficial effects besides accommodating the homeless - e.g. especially in rural areas where a reasonable increase in population could assist in supporting a secure future to local facilities such as clinics, schools and shops and of course the opportunity for employment that they offer

    THE HIGH COST OF BUILDING
    Following the recent Pandemic the cost of building has rocketed. Materials are more expensive and labour is in short supply. Since Brexit many European workers have left the UK. In 2019 there were 176,000 EU born building workers in the country. By 2020 this figure had dropped to 127,000. This is a drop of 28%. There is no doubt that this reduction will result in increased labour costs

    Use prefabrication to increase construction efficiency and reduce costs. Various methods can be used-eg-production of large partially finished wall, roof and floor panels, made in factories that have the capacity for mass production. The components are then transported before being assembled and finished on site. Alternatively entire room pods or capsules can also be made offsite before being transported and assembled. This is the proposed method for a typical affordable Hedge House shown as an example in this brochure
    Prefabrication using pods
    VEGETATION Using the wonderful variety of creepers and climbers that the plant kingdom can offer is ideal for covering prefabricated buildings. With the external walls protected by a “Troughwall”,the range of suitable plants can be greatly extended. The typically rather brutal and harsh appearance of such prefabricated structures can be softened and made more attractive-indeed transformed -by using the appropriate plant. Hopefully the close proximity of inspiring vegetation will provoke curiosity and exchange of information between neighbours-rather like what happens between dog owners and indeed gardeners on allotments! We believe therefore that living in a Prefabricated Hedge house will help break the ice between neighbours and this will be an important factor in building a strong community spirit.
    Vegetation plays a crucial part in protecting the Earth and preserving its balance so calamities are Minimised-this is what happens when that balance is lost

    A PLANET THAT IS IN TROUBLE
    With massive fires and floods, more frequent and more destructive weather, and with the loss of many species of plants and animals the current threats to the planet are the most extreme that have occurred for centuries, possibly millennia.

    All housing anywhere in the world, including the Hedge house should be built to THE PASSIVHAUS STANDARDS - that is with massive insulation, PV Solar panels,and for example internal Temperatures conserved with heat recovery systems etc. That way accommodation could use minimal energy especially that generated by remote or centralised power stations.Not only that, but with plentiful vegetation, The Prefabricated Hedge house can mop up substantial quantities of dirt and dust. With its absorbent surfaces large amounts of rainfall can also be retained on site and released slowly to ease the risk of flooding. But above all the vegetation could absorb CO2 and release O2. All in all prefabricated Hedge houses should produce a thoroughly benign habitat for plants and thus the Planet and its inhabitants, both animal and human.
